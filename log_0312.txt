3. mass and energy balances
solver with Ordinary Differential Equations for changes:
Mass Balance - How concentration changes based on reaction rates
Energy Balance- Tracks temperature changes ($dT/dt$) based on the heat of reaction ($Q_{rxn}$) and any heating/cooling from the reactor jacket ($Q_{ht}$).

reaction rate: r_i = k_i * Π (C_k ^ α_i,k) for k in R_i
Mass Balance: dC_j / dt = Σ (ν_i,j * r_i) from i=1 to 4
ν_i,j (Nu): The stoichiometric coefficient. It is negative if the component is a reactant (consumed) and positive if it is a product (generated)

5. sensitivity 
what extent the states (molar concentrations $C_j$) depend on the parameters ($\theta_p$).
$$S(t) \equiv \frac{dC_j}{d\theta_p} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_{comp} \times n_{param}}$$

The Objective Function (Weighted Sum of Squared Errors$) value the optimizer tries to minimize to find the best-fit parameters.
$$\min_{\theta} WSSE = (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_{model})^T \mathbf{W}_y^{-1} (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_{model})$$

3. The Gradient ($\nabla$)
The gradient guides the optimizer's search by using the Sensitivity Matrix calculated when eval_sens=True.

$$\nabla_{\theta_p} = \frac{dWSSE}{d\theta_p}$$
